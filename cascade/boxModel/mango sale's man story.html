THE MANGO SALE'S MAN STORY

Ravi was a hardworking mango seller in a small village. Every summer, he bought fresh mangoes from nearby farms and sold them in the town market. People liked Ravi because he was honest, friendly, and always sold good-quality fruit. One morning, Ravi woke up before sunrise. He carefully selected the best mangoes from a farmer's orchard. The mangoes were ripe, sweet, and golden yellow. He loaded them into his cart and headed to the market. When Ravi reached the market, he arranged the mangoes neatly. Soon, customers began to arrive. A woman stopped at his stall and asked, “How much do these mangoes cost?” Ravi smiled and replied, “They are very sweet and fresh. You can taste one before buying.” The woman tasted a mango and was impressed by its flavor. She bought two kilograms. After that, many other customers came to Ravi's stall. By noon, he had sold more than half of his stock. As the day became hotter, Ravi noticed an old man sitting nearby. The old man looked tired and thirsty. Ravi offered him some water and a ripe mango. The old man thanked him and said, “Your kindness will bring you success.” Ravi smiled and continued his work. Later that afternoon, a wealthy businessman visited the market. He wanted to buy mangoes in large quantities for his fruit shop in the city. The businessman checked several stalls but was not satisfied with the quality of the fruit. When he reached Ravi's stall, he carefully examined the mangoes. He found them fresh, clean, and perfectly ripe. “Can you supply mangoes to my shop every week?” the businessman asked. Ravi was surprised and excited. “Yes, I can,” he replied. The businessman placed a large order. It was the biggest sale Ravi had ever made. Ravi thanked him and promised to deliver the best mangoes every week. From that day onward, Ravi's business grew steadily. He worked hard, maintained quality, and treated every customer with respect. His reputation spread to nearby towns and cities. More shop owners started buying mangoes from him. A few years later, Ravi owned a successful fruit business. He employed several workers and helped local farmers sell their produce at fair prices. Even though he became successful, he never forgot his humble beginnings. He continued to be kind and honest with everyone he met. One day, Ravi saw the same old man who had blessed him years earlier. Ravi thanked him for his encouraging words. The old man smiled and said, “Success comes to those who work hard, stay honest, and help others.” Ravi realized that the old man's words were true. His success was not only because of the mangoes he sold but also because of his honesty, dedication, and kindness.

Moral:

Hard work, honesty, and kindness are the keys to lasting success
